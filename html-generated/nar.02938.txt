      March 4, 1871.
      Hon. J. A. J. Creswell,
      Postmaster General.
      Sir:
      I have the honor to acknowledge the receipt of your letter of the 3d instant, in relation
        to the case of Ferdinand Kohl, against the  Postmaster at Williamsburg, N. Y., and the apparent delay in the District
        Attorney in bringing the matter to a decision.
      I have informed that officer of your complaint, and have directed him to bring the case to
        an early, and, if possible, to a favorable termination, without unnecessary delay.
      post office case F. Kohl N. Y.
      Very respectfully, &amp;c.
      A. T. Akerman,
      Attorney General.
