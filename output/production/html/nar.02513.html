Oct. 13, 1871. Messrs. Stevens & Haynes, Law Publishers, London, W. C., England Sirs: I desire to order, through you, for this Department, to complete the series of English Reports, in our Library, the following: In House of Lords. Dow, 6 Vols. Bligh, 3 Vols. Bligh, N. S., 10 Vols. In Privy Council. Knapp, 3 Vols. Moore, 14 Vols. Moore, N. S. 3 Vols. In Chancery. Wilson. Jacob and Walker, 2 Vols. Jacob. Turner and Russell. Russell; 5 Vols. Russell and Milne, 2 Vols. Mylne and Keen, 3 Vols. Mylne and Craig, 5 Vols. Craig and Phillips. Phillips, 2 Vols. Hall and Twells, 2 Vols. Tamlyn Keene, 2 Vols. Beavan, 34 Vols. Simons and Stuart, 2 Vols. Simons, 17 Vols. Simons, N. S. 2 Vols. Drewry, 4 Vols. Drewry and Small, 2 Vols. Younge and Collyer, 2 Vols. Collyer, 2 Vols. De Gex and Small, 5 Vols. Small and Gifford, 3 Vols. Gifford, 3 Vols. Hare, 11 Vols. Kay. Kay and Johnson, 4 Vols. H. R. V. Johnson, Johnson and Hemming, 2 Vols. Hemming and Miller, 2 Vols. In King's Bench Maule and Selwyn, 6 Vols. Barnewall and Alderson, 5 Vols. Barnewall and Creeswell, 10 Vols. Barnewall and Adolphus, 5 Vols. Adolphus and Ellis, 12 Vols. In Common Pleas. Broderip and Bingham, 3 Vols. Bingham, 10 Vols. Bingham, N. Pas. 6 Vols. In Exchequer. Price, 13 Vols. Tyrrwhitt, 5 Vols. In Admiralty Swabey, Lushington, 2 Vols. Browning and Lushington. Crown cases. Cox's Crown Cases, 9 Vols. In Ireland The best series of Reports, containing the judgments of Lord Chancellor Sugden. I should like also to have you send me all the volumes that have been published of the present series of Irish Reports, and to continue them annually, when the volumes are bound, as I do not wish them to come in numbers. I am not particular in regard to binding, so long as it is strong and serviceable. Will you be good enough to have any books that you may have bound for me stamped "Department of Justice." You will please box these up carefully, direct them "Department of Justice, Washington, Care of the Marshal of the United States, New York," and send your bill to me. I had the pleasure of making the acquaintance of your Mr. Haynes two or three years ago in Boston, and he very likely remembers me. I am, Very respectfully, Clement Hugh Hill, Assistant Attorney General.
