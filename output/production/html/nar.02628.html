Nov. 28, 1871. Wm. Penn. Clark, Esq. No. 525 13th street, N. W. Washington, D. C. Sir: In answer to your letters of August 3d and Nov. 25th, 1871, I have to say that the papers transmitted in the case of John Murphy, alias Matthew Lauman, for whom a pardon is asked for the offence of passing counterfeit money, do not show such facts as justify me in recommending to the President a remission of the sentence. Very respectfully, A. T. Akerman, Attorney General.
pardon declined
