March 8, 1870. Hon. Hamilton Fish, Secretary of State. Sir: I send to you herewith the papers in the case of Wm. Currie of Mo., who has applied for relief from the penalty of a bail bond. The circumstances of the case seem to me to be such as would make a compromise of the claims against him on behalf of the United States extremely proper—and I submit the papers and the case to your favorable consideration. Very respectfully, E. R. Hoar, Attorney General.
Case of Wm. Currie, Mo.
