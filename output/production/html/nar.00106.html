Sept. 25, 1866 Hon. Hugh McCulloch, Secretary of the Treasury, Sir: I have received yours of the 10th, enclosing a copy of an opinion, delivered by Judge Hawkins, of Tennessee, holding so much of the Internal Revenue Act of June 30, 1864, as imposes a stamp tax upon Writs, & other original process issued by State Courts to be unconstitutional. Upon a careful consideration of this opinion, I adhere to the position expressed in my letter to you of September 1st, that this portion of the Act is valid & constitutional. It would be a useless waste of time to go at large into this question, as there is only one course by which it can be set at rest, & this is, by instituting prosecution, as I have advised in my former letter. I have the honor, &c. Henry Stanbery Attorney General
